Shattered: Repair Your Identity

A narrative exploration prototype built for Global Game Jam 2020 (Theme: Repair). The player wakes in an empty house with no recollection of self. By exploring rooms, recovering fragments, and listening for audio cues, they reconstruct who they were — and who they might become.

Context

  • Jam constraints: 48h build, theme-driven (“Repair”), minimal assets.
  • Goal to test environmental storytelling without heavy UI or VO.
  • Solo audio pipeline using Audacity; quick iteration on loops and stingers.

Goals

  • Deliver a coherent loop: explore → find fragment → unlock memory → progress.
  • Use sound to create tension and orientation without jump scares.
  • Ship a playable slice with a beginning, midpoint reveal, and resolution.

Approach

  • Spatial storytelling via room semantics (kitchen, study, bedroom) tied to identity facets.
  • Collectible “fragments” populate a timeline; each fragment unlocks a short prose memory.
  • Audio layers: base ambience, proximity hum for interactables, one-shot stingers for reveals.
  • Low-contrast UI; interaction affordances handled in-world to avoid HUD clutter.

Responsibilities

  • Narrative beats and scene sequencing.
  • Interaction rules and exploration pacing.
  • Sound design: ambience beds, foley, stingers; mix and attenuation curves.
  • Playtest loops and tuning under jam constraints.

System Designs

Fragments & Rooms

  • Each room gates 1–2 fragments with light spatial puzzles.
  • Fragments unlock micro-memories; three aligned memories unlock a new area.
  • Endgame “mirror” scene replays key audio motifs to resolve the arc.

Audio as Guidance

  • Proximity cue increases in volume and stereo width near interactables.
  • Minor-key stingers reserved for narrative reveals to avoid cue fatigue.
  • Dynamic low-pass when reading memories to focus attention.

Gallery

Outcome

  • Submitted a complete vertical slice with start–finish arc within jam time.
  • Playtests confirmed audio cues reduced aimless wandering and improved flow.
  • Post-jam interest to expand into branching memories and alternate endings.

Next Steps

  • Branching memory graph with soft locks and optional fragments.
  • Diegetic map journal to track discovered facets of identity.
  • Expanded sound palette with adaptive reverb by room size/materials.
